Spectator



Watching with hands shouting gale winds at mounds, down the line, between the bags, with visions of one’s small self or smaller self in combat confronted by another pawn of one’s existence.

The unexplained flood that clasps steel wires bent around flesh through which one stares
Down or up the balancing act that trembles on future throws.

In the throws, pounding heart moored in place washed over

By waves so easy at a distance without stake
A claim on another’s and others in line for tomorrow’s prize if today’s sorrows quelch Casey’s moment.

Batted about not unlike prosecutor’s court where generations culminate.

Alone
Alone
Alone

Unnursed and naked

Abandoned.

Flailing progenitors lost on the river flowing
Up the mountain of dreams
In search of promises

For what?

Honey’s milk suckled so sweet on small days
When every touch grasped new worlds of wonder
Primary reds, yellows, blues sang lullabies from moment to moment

Then down the slide with those pushed too far into the night
In the darkness the monster clasps steel wires but does not know why
Looking through, in or out, waiting for the break of day

Light touch fingers curved threads red in a twisting hurl on which sunrises ahead sink
Across pastime’s sand
In bags eyes
Say yes to spike and spite

Go
Go
Go

The omnipresent echo to get there from there.
Lost in a wilderness made by others’ hands

Safe from the descending storm
For now
Relieved
From the weight foisted from after to before to after
And after
Mirror clouds swing and collide time pendulums
The clocks tick by begging to advance
Scoring pages that make easy rips in the hands of the Spectator.
